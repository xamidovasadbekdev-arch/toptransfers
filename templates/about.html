– ABOUT US –

The 'TopFootballTransfers' website is engaged in predicting the transfer value of football players.

Here, 'transfer value' of a football player means the amount of compensation for breaking an ongoing employment contract with the player. Club 'A' breaks the existing contract with a player, and Club 'B' pays the compensation. After that, Club 'B' signs a new contract with the player. Why do clubs pay large sums of money for players? Because they want this player to play for them and do not want to wait until the end of his current contract. Big clubs sign more powerful players, while other clubs earn by searching for and developing the skills of new talent.

How to assess a footballer's transfer value?

Skills and mastery of the player. A player with better skills costs more.

The player's age. The younger the player, the higher the transfer value. A club pays compensation for a football player to sign a new contract and spreads transfer expenses over the new contract's duration. The shorter the possible term of a new contract, the less compensation clubs are willing to pay. Young players can also renew their contracts and stay at a club longer. Older players are often not on the market, making it harder to recover transfer expenses. Younger players have greater potential for growth in skill and physical condition, while older players are more prone to injury and recurring health issues.

Contract term. The expiry date of the current contract also affects transfer value. If the contract expires before the next transfer window, there is essentially no transfer value — there is no reason to pay for a player who can be signed as a free agent in the next window. If only one transfer window remains before expiry (especially a winter window), the transfer value will be low. The more time remaining on the contract, the higher the transfer value.

Other factors can also affect transfer value. For example, transfers from clubs in the top five championships (Spain, England, Italy, Germany, France) are usually higher than transfers from other leagues. Transfers of players from non-European championships (except Brazil and Argentina) are often worth very little. Portuguese and Dutch clubs, for instance, frequently sign free players from non-European or Eastern European leagues and sell them on to bigger clubs. The largest transfers typically occur between top clubs.

Another factor is the positive or negative media profile of a player. A footballer who avoids incidents (especially criminal or sexual) is more in demand. Players with a negative media profile tend to have a lower transfer value. Influential agents (such as Raiola and Mendes) can also push clubs to spend more. No club wants to fall out with an agent who represents many talented players they may need in the future.

Is there a mathematical formula for calculating a player's transfer value? No. Predictions are based on the factors listed above, analysis of past transfers, and monitoring trends in the rising transfer market. Note that the transfer values shown here do not account for the Chinese factor. After analyzing transfers involving Chinese clubs, we could not find consistent correlations with the usual influencing factors. Very few players want to play in China, so Chinese clubs are forced to overpay.

To comply with Financial Fair Play rules, some clubs resort to workarounds — first taking a player on loan, then buying him out at the end of the loan period. The loan agreement, transfer agreement, and employment contract are concluded simultaneously. Therefore, when calculating transfer costs, we add the loan fee to the transfer fee if the player was previously on loan from the selling club.

In some cases, clubs arrange future transfers — contracts stating that a player will move to another club at a set future date. In these cases, we register the transfer in the "Latest Transfers" section and move the player to the new club's list immediately, even if he is still playing for his previous club. Players are listed under the club that owns their contract, regardless of where they are currently competing.

It should be noted that our website only covers top transfers — those amounting to more than 20 million euros. This is because the majority of football fans are interested in major transfers and famous names. Also, focusing on a limited number of players allows our team to monitor and update information more promptly and accurately.

What is TopFootballTransfers.com for? To give fans clearer information about player transfer costs. Many sites specialize in determining player market value, but the figures shown often diverge greatly from the actual transfer amounts. This is because a footballer is not a commodity — there is no fixed market value. There is only the transfer value: the compensation amount for breaking a contract. Mathematical formulas alone cannot show the full picture, because many factors beyond a player's skill level influence the final figure. We rely heavily on consultations with football agents, as they are professionally involved in transfers. Our approach allows us to accurately predict the transfer value of players.
